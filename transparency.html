Transparency Framework

Trust through responsibility — not exposure

Donate Explore Tokenomics

Transparency in B2H exists to protect trust — not to expose the vulnerable, enable exploitation, or invite harm.

Why Transparency Has Limits

Absolute transparency is not always ethical. In humanitarian work, exposure can cause risk, misuse, and real-world harm.

B2H rejects transparency theater. Responsibility matters more than visibility.

What B2H Commits to Disclose

  • Mission purpose and ethical constraints
  • Governance structure and safeguards
  • Participation principles and risk warnings
  • High-level humanitarian impact summaries
  • Use-of-funds categories (non-exploitable)

What B2H Will Not Publicly Disclose

  • Exact wallet addresses or internal accounts
  • Detailed logistics of aid distribution
  • Personal identities of beneficiaries
  • Security-sensitive operational data
  • Private continuity designations

Withholding information protects people — not power.

Financial Communication Boundaries

B2H does not publish price targets, profit projections, or speculative forecasts.

Financial transparency exists only within ethical and humanitarian boundaries.

Use of Funds — Ethical Disclosure

  • Humanitarian assistance
  • Operational sustainability
  • Security, compliance, and protection
  • Minimal logistics and coordination

Excess, luxury, or extraction is explicitly prohibited.

Privacy as Protection

Public exposure of suffering leads to stigma, targeting, and exploitation.

B2H protects dignity by refusing to turn suffering into content.

Independent Accountability (When Safe)

Where feasible and safe, trusted ethical reviewers may privately verify practices.

No reviewer may claim control, influence, or authority.

Handling Misinformation

Any individual claiming guaranteed returns, inside access, or special authority does not represent B2H.

Silence in response to hype is intentional.

Amanah (Trust)

In Islamic accountability, amanah is sacred.

Trust is broken not only by lies, but by careless truth that causes harm.

Final Principle

B2H will always choose responsible restraint over harmful exposure.

Transparency serves people — people do not serve transparency.
